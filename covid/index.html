What we can do now

Wear a quality mask. Concerningly, the latest variant is highly transmissible outdoors, so yes, it will be effective to wear outdoors as well. During my summer in Japan, everyone masked indoors and outdoors even in 90-100+ F temperatures, even after the government loosened outdoor masking requirements. While I don‚Äôt dream that we can change the American attitude towards masking overnight, I hope we can start to see it as a way to protect others than as an inconvenience.

And for those on BCBS MA, they are able to send 8 free COVID test kits per month, details available here: https://www.bluecrossma.org/myblue/at-home-covid-test-coverage

Even with testing, beware that negative test results are not bulletproof - many (including myself) have tested negative on tests for several days into the rise of COVID symptoms.

If you have been infected or suffer from long COVID

I of course do not provide these resources to blame or shame anyone who has been infected. Long COVID patients face stigma on top of suffering the symptoms. Many long COVID patients are not believed by their own family members, friends, and doctors, which is why we may not feel we know many people who have it. Resources such as Survivor Corps exist. I would love to know about more resources on how to better support this community, but I hope this page is a start.
